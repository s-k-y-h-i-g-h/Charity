## 🚀 Registration Process (Online via Companies House)

### Step 1: Go to Companies House Web Incorporation Service
**URL:** https://www.gov.uk/register-a-community-interest-company

**OR** use third-party formation service (faster, more expensive):
- CIC Tools: https://www.cictools.co.uk (£65 + their fee)
- 1st Formations: https://www.1stformations.co.uk (£395 all-in)
- Coddan: https://www.coddan.co.uk (£215 all-in)

**Recommendation:** Use official GOV.UK service (£65) if you're comfortable with the process. Use formation service (£215-395) if you want hand-holding.

### Step 2: Create Companies House Account
- Email address (create new one for business?)
- Password
- Verify phone number (your mobile)

### Step 3: Start Web Incorporation (Form IN01)

**Section 1: Company Details**
- Company name: `Longevity Access CIC`
- Company type: Community Interest Company (CIC)
- Limited by: Shares
- Registered office: 19 Elderflower Way, [Postcode]
- SIC codes (what the company does):
  - 72190: Other research and experimental development on natural sciences
  - 85590: Other education n.e.c. (education component)
  - 94990: Membership organizations n.e.c. (if doing advocacy)

**Section 2: Share Capital**
- Total shares: 100
- Nominal value: £1 each
- Total capital: £100
- Allocation:
  - SkyHigh: 1 share
  - [Stepdad]: 1 share
  - [Parent]: 1 share
  - Remaining 97 shares: unallocated (for future fundraising)

**Section 3: Directors**
- Director 1: SkyHigh (DOB, NI number, address)
- Director 2: [Stepdad Name] (DOB, NI number, address)
- Director 3: [Parent Name] (DOB, NI number, address)
- All directors appointed on: [Registration date]

**Section 4: Memorandum & Articles**
- Upload: `longevity-access-articles.md` (formatted as PDF)
- Or use CIC Model Articles (simpler, less control)

**Section 5: Community Interest Statement**
- Upload: `longevity-access-cic36.md` (formatted as PDF)
- Or copy/paste content into web form

**Section 6: Person with Significant Control (PSC)**
- You're the founder, but CICs have asset lock
- Usually no PSC (or list directors as PSCs)
- Check CIC guidance on this

**Section 7: Declaration**
- Confirm information is accurate
- Confirm company will operate for community interest
- Sign electronically

### Step 4: Pay £65 Fee
- Card payment
- Receipt emailed

### Step 5: Wait for Approval
- **Timeline:** 3 working days (usually faster)
- **Email notification:** Company number assigned
- **Certificate of Incorporation:** Download from Companies House

## 📧 Post-Registration (Within 14 Days)

### ✅ Register for Corporation Tax
**URL:** https://www.gov.uk/register-for-corporation-tax

- Within 3 months of starting business activities
- Need: Company number, registration date, accounting period end date
- Set up online HMRC account for company

### ✅ Set Up Business Bank Account
**Recommended banks for CICs:**
- Metro Bank (CIC-friendly)
- Starling Bank (digital, free business account)
- Tide (good for startups)
- Barclays (traditional, more fees)

**Documents needed:**
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Memorandum & Articles
- Director IDs (passport/driving license)
- Proof of address (utility bill)

**Note:** Some banks require CIC to be trading for 3 months before opening account. Plan accordingly.

### ✅ Set Up Accounting System
**Options:**
- Free: Excel/Google Sheets template
- Low-cost: Xero (£15/month), QuickBooks (£20/month)
- CIC-specific: FreeAgent (integrates with HMRC)

**Track:**
- Income (grants, donations, earned revenue)
- Expenses (subsidies, staff, hosting, legal)
- VAT (register if turnover >£90,000/year)
- PAYE (if paying staff)

## 📝 Post-Registration (Within 3 Months)

### ✅ First Annual Accounts
- Prepare full accounts (even if no trading yet)
- File with Companies House within 9 months of year-end
- File CIC Report (CIC30) with Regulator within 9 months

### ✅ First Accounting Period
- Corporation Tax return due 12 months after accounting period ends
- Payment due 9 months + 1 day after period ends

### ✅ Set Up Grant Applications
- Wellcome Trust (rolling deadline)
- Age UK (check application rounds)
- Impetus Grants (check deadlines)
- UKRI Innovation Fund

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Wrong SIC codes:** Use research/education codes, not retail/manufacturing
2. **Missing CIC36:** Must submit community interest statement
3. **Wrong company type:** Select "Community Interest Company", not "Private Limited Company"
4. **Address format:** Must be full UK address with postcode
5. **Share allocation:** Don't allocate all 100 shares to founders (keep room for fundraising)
6. **PSC confusion:** CICs have asset lock, so PSC rules differ from regular companies
