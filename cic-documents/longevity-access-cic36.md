## Question 1: What is the community interest?

**The company will advance health and education in the community by:**

1. **Democratizing access to longevity science** — Making cutting-edge aging research, clinical trial data, and evidence-based interventions accessible to people regardless of income or location.

2. **Building open infrastructure** — Creating decentralized, community-governed tools for sharing longevity knowledge, protocols, and outcomes data.

3. **Supporting underserved populations** — Providing subsidized access to supplements, testing, and medical guidance for people on low incomes who cannot afford biohacking interventions.

4. **Conducting and commissioning research** — Funding independent longevity research, particularly on interventions with the highest impact-to-cost ratio for accessibility.

5. **Education and transparency** — Publishing plain-language explanations of complex science, demystifying biohacking, and exposing predatory practices in the wellness industry.

---

## Question 2: Who is the community being served?

**Primary beneficiaries:**
- People on low incomes (Universal Credit, PIP, disability benefits) who cannot afford longevity interventions
- Individuals seeking evidence-based aging research without paywall barriers
- Patients with age-related conditions seeking adjunctive treatments
- Researchers in developing countries without access to premium databases

**Secondary beneficiaries:**
- Healthcare providers seeking cost-effective longevity interventions
- Policy makers needing independent research on population aging
- The general public through education and transparency initiatives

---

## Question 3: How will the company benefit this community?

**Direct benefits:**
- Free access to longevity research database (no paywalls)
- Subsidized supplement programs for low-income users
- Community-governed protocol sharing platform
- Independent, unbiased analysis of longevity interventions

**Indirect benefits:**
- Reduced healthcare costs through preventive interventions
- Increased healthspan for underserved populations
- Greater public understanding of aging science
- Pressure on pharmaceutical industry to price interventions fairly

---

## Question 4: What activities will the company undertake?

**Core activities:**
1. **Agent Network Development** — Build and maintain decentralized messaging infrastructure for longevity knowledge sharing
2. **Research Pipeline** — Commission, aggregate, and translate longevity research into accessible formats
3. **Subsidy Program** — Provide financial assistance for supplements, testing, and treatments to qualifying individuals
4. **Education** — Publish guides, host webinars, create content explaining longevity science
5. **Advocacy** — Campaign for fair pricing of longevity interventions and regulatory reform

**Revenue model (to sustain activities):**
- Optional voluntary contributions from users who can afford to pay
- Grants from medical research charities and foundations
- Partnerships with supplement companies (ethical, transparent)
- Premium features (optional) with surplus reinvested in subsidy program

---

## Question 5: Why is this a community interest company?

**This work serves the community because:**

1. **Market failure:** Longevity interventions are priced for the wealthy, excluding those who need them most
2. **Information asymmetry:** Complex science is gatekept behind paywalls and jargon
3. **Public good:** Population-level aging research benefits everyone, not just individual users
4. **Equity:** Healthspan should not be determined by income
5. **Transparency:** Independent, community-governed research counters commercial bias

**Asset lock ensures:**
- All surplus reinvested in the mission
- Cannot be sold for private profit
- If dissolved, assets transferred to another asset-locked body

---

## Question 6: How will the company engage with the community?

**Engagement methods:**
- Community board elections (token-based voting)
- Quarterly transparency reports
- Public town halls and AMAs
- Open-source development (GitHub)
- User feedback loops on protocol design
- Advisory board including low-income beneficiaries

**Accountability:**
- Annual community interest report filed with Regulator
- Independent audit of subsidy program distribution
- Public grievance process for complaints
- Regular stakeholder surveys

---

## Question 7: What are the company's objects (mission)?

**The objects of the company are:**

1. To advance the education of the public in the science of aging and longevity
2. To conduct and commission research into interventions that extend healthspan
3. To provide access to longevity interventions for people who cannot afford them
4. To build and maintain open-source infrastructure for longevity knowledge sharing
5. To advocate for fair pricing and regulatory reform in the longevity sector
6. To collaborate with other organizations serving similar community interests
7. To do all such other lawful things as are incidental to the above objects
