## Question 1: What is the community interest?

**The company will advance human potential and education in the community by:**

1. **Democratizing cognitive enhancement** — Making evidence-based nootropics, cognitive training, and mental performance interventions accessible to people regardless of income.

2. **Building open infrastructure** — Creating decentralized, community-governed tools for sharing cognitive enhancement protocols, outcomes data, and research.

3. **Supporting underserved populations** — Providing subsidized access to cognitive enhancement interventions for people with ADHD, brain fog, depression, or age-related cognitive decline who cannot afford treatment.

4. **Conducting and commissioning research** — Funding independent research on cognitive enhancement, particularly interventions with high impact-to-cost ratios.

5. **Education and safety** — Publishing evidence-based guidance on cognitive enhancement, exposing predatory practices, and promoting safe, responsible use.

---

## Question 2: Who is the community being served?

**Primary beneficiaries:**
- People on low incomes with cognitive challenges (ADHD, brain fog, depression)
- Students and professionals seeking cognitive enhancement without financial barriers
- Older adults experiencing age-related cognitive decline
- Researchers in cognitive science without access to premium resources

**Secondary beneficiaries:**
- Mental health providers seeking adjunctive interventions
- Educational institutions looking for evidence-based cognitive support
- The general public through education and safety initiatives

---

## Question 3: How will the company benefit this community?

**Direct benefits:**
- Free access to cognitive enhancement research database
- Subsidized nootropic protocols for qualifying individuals
- Community-governed protocol sharing platform
- Independent, unbiased analysis of cognitive interventions

**Indirect benefits:**
- Improved educational and employment outcomes for underserved populations
- Reduced mental health burden through preventive interventions
- Greater public understanding of cognitive science
- Pressure on pharmaceutical industry to price cognitive treatments fairly

---

## Question 4: What activities will the company undertake?

**Core activities:**
1. **Agent Network Development** — Build and maintain decentralized messaging infrastructure for cognitive enhancement knowledge sharing (shared with Longevity Access CIC)
2. **Research Pipeline** — Commission, aggregate, and translate cognitive enhancement research
3. **Subsidy Program** — Provide financial assistance for nootropics, testing, and cognitive interventions
4. **Education** — Publish guides on cognitive enhancement, safety, and responsible use
5. **Advocacy** — Campaign for fair pricing and regulatory reform in cognitive health sector

**Revenue model (to sustain activities):**
- Optional voluntary contributions from users who can afford to pay
- Grants from mental health and education charities
- Partnerships with cognitive health companies (ethical, transparent)
- Premium features (optional) with surplus reinvested in subsidy program

---

## Question 5: Why is this a community interest company?

**This work serves the community because:**

1. **Market failure:** Cognitive enhancement is priced for the wealthy, excluding those who need it most
2. **Information asymmetry:** Complex neuroscience is gatekept behind paywalls
3. **Public good:** Cognitive health benefits society through improved education, employment, and mental health
4. **Equity:** Cognitive potential should not be determined by income
5. **Safety:** Independent, community-governed research counters commercial hype and dangerous advice

**Asset lock ensures:**
- All surplus reinvested in the mission
- Cannot be sold for private profit
- If dissolved, assets transferred to another asset-locked body

---

## Question 6: How will the company engage with the community?

**Engagement methods:**
- Community board elections (token-based voting)
- Quarterly transparency reports
- Public town halls and AMAs
- Open-source development (GitHub)
- User feedback loops on protocol design
- Advisory board including beneficiaries

**Accountability:**
- Annual community interest report filed with Regulator
- Independent audit of subsidy program distribution
- Public grievance process for complaints
- Regular stakeholder surveys

---

## Question 7: What are the company's objects (mission)?

**The objects of the company are:**

1. To advance the education of the public in cognitive science and mental performance
2. To conduct and commission research into cognitive enhancement interventions
3. To provide access to cognitive enhancement for people who cannot afford them
4. To build and maintain open-source infrastructure for cognitive knowledge sharing
5. To advocate for fair pricing and regulatory reform in the cognitive health sector
6. To collaborate with sister organizations (including Longevity Access CIC) on shared infrastructure
7. To do all such other lawful things as are incidental to the above objects
