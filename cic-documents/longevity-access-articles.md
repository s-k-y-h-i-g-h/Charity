## ARTICLES OF ASSOCIATION

### PART 1: INTERPRETATION

**1. Definitions**
- "The Act" means Companies Act 2006
- "The Regulator" means the Office of the Regulator of Community Interest Companies
- "Community Interest" means the benefit to the community as described in the CIC36 form
- "Asset Lock" means the restrictions on asset disposal as required for CICs
- "Sister Organization" means Cognitive Thrive CIC or any other CIC sharing infrastructure

**2. Model Articles**
These Articles adopt the Model Articles for Private Companies Limited by Shares (Schedule 1, Companies (Model Articles) Regulations 2008) with the following modifications for CIC requirements.

---

### PART 2: COMMUNITY INTEREST OBJECTS

**3. Objects**
The objects of the company are:

a) To advance the education of the public in the science of aging and longevity
b) To conduct and commission research into interventions that extend healthspan
c) To provide access to longevity interventions for people who cannot afford them
d) To build and maintain open-source infrastructure for longevity knowledge sharing
e) To advocate for fair pricing and regulatory reform in the longevity sector
f) To collaborate with Sister Organizations on shared infrastructure
g) To do all such other lawful things as are incidental to the above objects

**4. Community Interest Test**
Every decision of the company must be evaluated against whether it serves the Community Interest as defined in the CIC36 form. Directors must document this evaluation for significant decisions.

---

### PART 3: ASSET LOCK

**5. Statutory Asset Lock**
The company benefits from the statutory asset lock under Part 2 of the Companies (Audit, Investigations and Community Enterprise) Act 2004.

**6. Distribution Restrictions**
a) The company may not distribute assets to members except:
   - Dividends up to the statutory limit (currently 35% of paid-up share capital)
   - Interest on loans up to market rate
   - Payments for goods/services at market rate

b) All surplus beyond operational needs must be reinvested in the Community Interest.

**7. Dissolution**
If the company is dissolved, remaining assets must be transferred to:
- Another asset-locked body (CIC or charity)
- With similar Community Interest objects
- As approved by the Regulator

---

### PART 4: DIRECTORS

**8. Director Requirements**
a) Minimum 2 directors required
b) Maximum 7 directors
c) At least one director must be independent (not receiving remuneration)
d) Directors serve 3-year terms, renewable once

**9. Director Duties**
Directors must:
a) Act in the Community Interest, not personal interest
b) Declare any conflicts of interest
c) Attend at least 60% of board meetings
d) Complete annual director declaration of community interest commitment

**10. Community Director**
One director position shall be reserved for a beneficiary representative (person who has received subsidy support). Elected by community vote.

---

### PART 5: SHARES AND TRANSFER

**11. Share Structure**
a) Ordinary shares of £1 each
b) No preferential rights
c) One share = one vote (no weighted voting)

**12. Transfer Restrictions**
a) Shares may not be transferred without Board approval
b) Transfers must maintain the asset lock
c) Regulator must approve any transfer that would change control

**13. Share Capital**
Initial share capital: £100 (100 shares of £1 each)
Future capital increases require:
- Board resolution
- Member approval
- Regulator notification

---

### PART 6: GOVERNANCE

**14. Board Meetings**
a) Minimum 4 meetings per year
b) Quorum: 2 directors
c) Decisions by simple majority
d) Minutes recorded and retained for 6 years

**15. Annual General Meeting**
a) Must be held within 18 months of incorporation
b) Subsequent AGMs within 15 months of previous
c) All members entitled to attend and vote
d) Community interest report presented

**16. Community Engagement**
a) Quarterly transparency reports published publicly
b) Annual community survey conducted
c) Grievance process maintained and publicized
d) Community board elections held annually

---

### PART 7: FINANCIAL PROVISIONS

**17. Financial Year**
1st April to 31st March

**18. Accounts**
a) Full accounts prepared annually
b) Independent examination or audit as required by Companies House
c) Filed with Companies House and Regulator within 9 months of year-end

**19. Subsidy Program**
a) Minimum 20% of surplus allocated to subsidy program
b) Eligibility criteria published and transparent
c) Annual report on subsidy distribution

---

### PART 8: SISTER ORGANIZATION RELATIONSHIPS

**20. Shared Infrastructure**
The company may collaborate with Sister Organizations on:
a) Technology infrastructure (agent network, databases)
b) Joint research initiatives
c) Shared administrative functions
d) Coordinated advocacy

**21. Cross-Licensing**
Intellectual property developed for shared infrastructure shall be:
a) Licensed to Sister Organizations at no cost
b) Open-sourced where possible
c) Retained as community asset, not private property

**22. Governance Coordination**
a) Directors may sit on both boards (with conflict disclosure)
b) Joint strategy sessions held quarterly
c) Separate financial accounts maintained

---

### PART 9: AMENDMENTS

**23. Article Amendments**
a) Requires special resolution (75% member vote)
b) Regulator approval required for changes to:
   - Objects clause
   - Asset lock provisions
   - Community interest definition
c) Must maintain CIC status

---

### PART 10: WINDING UP

**24. Dissolution Process**
a) Members may propose dissolution by special resolution
b) Regulator must be notified
c) Asset transfer plan submitted to Regulator
d) Remaining assets transferred to asset-locked body with similar objects
