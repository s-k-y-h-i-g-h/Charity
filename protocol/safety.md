# Safety Notes

**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## General Principles

1. **Start low, go slow** — especially with Tier 2 and Tier 3 compounds. Your body will tell you what's too much.
2. **Track everything** — keep a log of what you take, when, and how you feel. Patterns emerge over weeks, not days.
3. **Blood tests before and after** — establish baselines. Monitor liver function, kidney function, lipid panels, fasting glucose. This is how you know if something's working or causing harm.
4. **Know your interactions** — some compounds potentiate others. Understand what you're combining before you combine it.
5. **Stop and assess** — if something feels wrong, pause the compound and reassess. Don't push through unexplained symptoms.

## Compound-Specific Safety Notes

### Tier 1 (Universal Foundation)

**Omega-3 fish oil**
- Generally very safe. High doses (>3g/day) may thin blood — caution if on anticoagulants or before surgery.
- Quality matters: look for products tested for heavy metals and oxidation.

**Vitamin D3 + K2**
- Fat-soluble: accumulates. Don't megadose without testing levels first.
- Test your baseline 25-OH-D before starting. Target range: 40-60 ng/mL for most people.

**Magnesium**
- Can cause loose stools at high doses — glycinate or threonate forms are gentler on the gut.
- Avoid if you have severe kidney disease.

**Probiotics**
- Generally safe. Start with a single strain or low diversity product if you have SIBO or histamine issues — high-diversity products can trigger temporary die-off symptoms.

### Tier 2 (Expanded Protocol)

**Curcumin**
- Potentiates some drugs — including blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin) and diabetes medications. Check before combining.
- Piperine enhances absorption — some people are sensitive to piperine.

**CoQ10**
- May reduce warfarin efficacy. Monitor INR if on blood thinners.
- Ubiquinol (reduced form) is better absorbed than ubiquinone, especially after 40.

**Alpha-lipoic acid**
- Can affect thyroid hormone levels — monitor if on thyroid medication.
- May affect blood glucose — beneficial for some, problematic for others with hypoglycemia.

**Resveratrol**
- May interact with blood thinners and hormone-sensitive conditions.
- Generally well-tolerated at 500mg/day.

**Spermidine**
- Still emerging evidence. Start at low end of dosing range.
- Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding — not enough safety data.

### Tier 3 (Advanced Protocols — Requires Supervision)

**Selegiline**
- MAO-B inhibition is selective at low doses (5-10mg). At higher doses, loses selectivity.
- Critical: watch for tyramine interactions at higher doses. The sublingual route at low doses largely avoids this, but know your sources.
- Do not combine with serotonergic drugs (SSRIs, tryptophan) without medical supervision — risk of serotonin syndrome at higher doses.

**Memantine**
-NMDA antagonist. Mechanism is well-characterized but individual response varies.
- Starting oral administration (March 2026) to reduce IM injection site infection risk. Oral bioavailability is approximately 100%.
- Watch for: dizziness, headache, constipation, anxiety.

**Berberine**
- Potent. Can cause GI upset, constipation, or diarrhea.
- Inhibits CYP3A4 — affects metabolism of many drugs. Check interactions.
- May lower blood pressure and blood glucose — monitor if on medications for either.

**Rapamycin**
- Immunosuppressive. This is a serious consideration.
- Not appropriate for anyone with active infection or compromised immune function.
- Requires baseline and ongoing monitoring by a physician.

**Senolytic combinations (fisetin, quercetin, dasatinib)**
- Dasatinib is a chemotherapy agent. Should only be used under oncological supervision.
- Fisetin and quercetin are generally well-tolerated but the combination protocol requires specific timing and fasting.

## Red Flags — Stop and Consult a Doctor

- Unexplained fatigue that doesn't resolve with rest
- Persistent headaches or visual disturbances
- Chest pain, palpitations, or irregular heartbeat
- Persistent GI symptoms (blood in stool, severe constipation, unexplained weight loss)
- Mood changes beyond typical adjustment — especially sudden shifts
- Any allergic reaction: rash, swelling, difficulty breathing
- Abnormal bleeding or bruising
- Jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes)

## Contraindications (General)

| Compound | Contraindication |
|---|---|
| Selegiline + serotonergic drugs | Serotonin syndrome risk |
| High-dose fish oil | Anticoagulant therapy |
| Resveratrol | Hormone-sensitive conditions |
| CoQ10 | On blood thinners (monitor INR) |
| Berberine | On other glucose-lowering drugs |
| Rapamycin | Active infection, immunosuppression |
| Spermidine | Pregnancy, breastfeeding |

---

## The Most Important Safety Rule

**If you're on prescribed medication, talk to your doctor before starting any Tier 2 or Tier 3 compound.** 

Many longevity compounds are pharmacologically active. They interact with prescription drugs in ways that matter. A 10-minute conversation with your GP or pharmacist can prevent serious complications.

---

*This is not an exhaustive safety guide. Research compounds thoroughly before use. When in doubt, don't — and ask a qualified professional.*
