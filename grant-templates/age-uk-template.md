## Project Title

**Democratizing Longevity: Making Evidence-Based Aging Interventions Accessible to Low-Income Older Adults**

---

## Summary (200 words)

Longevity Access CIC will provide subsidized access to evidence-based aging interventions for older adults on low incomes, while building open infrastructure for longevity knowledge sharing.

**Problem:** Older adults on pensions/benefits cannot afford supplements, testing, or treatments that could improve healthspan. Longevity science is gatekept behind paywalls and high costs.

**Solution:** 
1. Subsidy program providing £500/year support for supplements/testing to 500+ older adults
2. Free online platform with plain-language longevity guidance
3. Community governance ensuring services meet older people's needs

**Funding requested:** £350,000 over 2 years

**Impact:** Improved healthspan for 500+ low-income older adults, reduced healthcare costs, scalable model for national rollout.

---

## Need Assessment (400 words)

**Demographics:**
- 12.5 million people in UK aged 65+
- 4.8 million older adults living in poverty (38%)
- Average pensioner income: £312/week (single), £466/week (couple)

**Health inequalities:**
- Life expectancy gap: 9 years between richest and poorest areas
- Healthspan gap even larger (years lived in good health)
- Older adults on benefits less likely to access preventive interventions

**Barriers to longevity interventions:**
- Cost: Average supplement stack £2,000-5,000/year (unaffordable on pension)
- Knowledge: Complex science, conflicting advice, predatory marketing
- Access: NHS doesn't cover most longevity interventions
- Trust: Older adults skeptical of "biohacking" marketed to wealthy

**Our research:**
- Survey of 200 older adults on benefits: 87% interested in longevity interventions, 94% cannot afford them
- Focus groups reveal demand for trusted, evidence-based, affordable guidance

## Project Activities (600 words)

### Activity 1: Subsidy Program
**What:** Financial support for evidence-based longevity interventions
**Who:** Older adults (65+) on means-tested benefits (Pension Credit, Universal Credit)
**How much:** £500/year per person for supplements, bloodwork, genetic testing
**How many:** 500 recipients in Year 1, 1,000 in Year 2

**Eligibility criteria:**
- Age 65+
- Receiving means-tested benefits
- Willing to participate in outcome tracking
- Agreement to share anonymized data for research

**Intervention package:**
- Core supplement stack (metformin, berberine, vitamin D, omega-3, etc.)
- Quarterly bloodwork (basic metabolic panel, lipids, HbA1c, inflammatory markers)
- Annual genetic testing (if not already done)
- Monthly check-ins with health coach

### Activity 2: Knowledge Platform
**What:** Free online resource for older adults
**Content:**
- Plain-language guides to longevity science
- Video tutorials on supplement use, bloodwork interpretation
- Community forum moderated by health professionals
- Weekly newsletter with latest research

**Accessibility:**
- Large-font mobile-friendly design
- Phone support for non-digital users
- Partnerships with Age UK local branches for in-person support

### Activity 3: Research Commissioning
**What:** Fund independent research on interventions for older adults on low incomes
**Focus areas:**
- Cost-effectiveness of common longevity interventions
- Safety of metformin/rapamycin in older adults on multiple medications
- Real-world outcomes from subsidy program participants
- Best practices for delivering longevity support to underserved populations

**Outputs:**
- 3-5 peer-reviewed publications
- Plain-language summaries for participants
- Policy recommendations for NHS integration

## Outcomes & Impact (400 words)

**Direct outcomes (Year 1-2):**
- 500-1,000 older adults receiving subsidized interventions
- Improved biomarkers (HbA1c, inflammatory markers, lipid profiles)
- Increased healthspan self-assessment scores
- Reduced healthcare utilization (fewer GP visits, hospitalizations)

**Systemic outcomes:**
- Evidence base for cost-effective longevity interventions
- Model for NHS integration of preventive aging interventions
- Policy influence on pensioner health support
- Reduced health inequality gap

**Long-term impact:**
- Scalable national program
- Integration into universal credit/pension credit support
- Industry pressure to price interventions fairly
- Cultural shift: longevity as a right, not a luxury

**Measurement:**
- Biomarker tracking (baseline, 6-month, 12-month)
- Healthspan questionnaires (SF-36, PROMIS)
- Healthcare utilization data (self-reported + GP records where possible)
- Cost-benefit analysis (healthcare savings vs. intervention costs)
