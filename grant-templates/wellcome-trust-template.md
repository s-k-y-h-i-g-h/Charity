## Executive Summary (250 words max)

**Longevity Access CIC will democratize access to aging research and evidence-based interventions for underserved populations in the UK.**

Currently, longevity science is gatekept behind paywalls, expensive supplements, and medical systems that exclude low-income individuals. We're building open-source infrastructure to make aging research accessible to everyone, regardless of income.

**What we're requesting:** £750,000 over 3 years to:
1. Build a decentralized agent network for longevity knowledge sharing
2. Fund a subsidy program providing supplements/testing to 500+ low-income individuals
3. Commission independent research on cost-effective longevity interventions
4. Publish plain-language educational content reaching 50,000+ people

**Why this matters:** Population aging will cost the UK £X billion annually. Preventive interventions accessible to all could reduce this burden while improving healthspan for underserved populations.

**Our unique approach:** Community-governed, open-source, and explicitly focused on equity—not just research for the wealthy.

## The Problem (500 words)

**Longevity interventions are priced for the wealthy:**

- Average annual supplement stack for biohackers: £2,000-5,000
- Longevity research databases (PubMed Premium, Nature, Science): £300-1,000/year
- Genetic testing + interpretation: £500-2,000
- Bloodwork + biomarker tracking: £1,000-3,000/year

**This excludes:**
- 14 million people in the UK living in poverty
- People on Universal Credit/PIP (like our founder)
- Older adults on fixed incomes
- Ethnic minorities with lower average wealth

**Information asymmetry worsens the problem:**
- Complex science locked behind paywalls
- Predatory wellness industry preys on vulnerable people
- No centralized, unbiased source for evidence-based longevity guidance

**The consequence:** Healthspan inequality. Wealthy people live longer, healthier lives through access to interventions that are simply unavailable to those who need them most.

---

## Our Solution (750 words)

**Longevity Access CIC provides three core services:**

### 1. Open Knowledge Infrastructure
- **Decentralized agent network** for sharing longevity protocols and outcomes
- **Free research database** aggregating open-access papers, clinical trials, and meta-analyses
- **Plain-language translations** of complex science
- **Community governance** ensuring content reflects beneficiary needs, not commercial interests

### 2. Subsidy Program
- **Direct financial support** for supplements, testing, and treatments
- **Eligibility:** UK residents on means-tested benefits
- **Target:** 500 individuals in Year 1, 2,000 by Year 3
- **Average support:** £500/year per person (covers essential stack)
- **Outcome tracking:** Longitudinal healthspan metrics (self-reported + biomarkers where possible)

### 3. Independent Research Commissioning
- **Focus:** Cost-effective interventions with highest accessibility potential
- **Examples:** Metformin protocols, rapamycin dosing, senolytic natural compounds
- **Partnerships:** Universities, independent researchers, clinical trial networks
- **Output:** Open-access publications, plain-language summaries

**Why this approach works:**
- **Scalable:** Agent network grows organically through community adoption
- **Sustainable:** Grants + voluntary contributions from wealthy users subsidize low-income access
- **Transparent:** All spending, outcomes, and decisions published publicly
- **Community-governed:** Beneficiaries have voting power on priorities
