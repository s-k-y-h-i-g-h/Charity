## The Problem We're Solving (150 words)

**Longevity interventions are stuck in the lab.**

Scientists discover promising compounds (rapamycin, senolytics, NAD+ boosters), but:
1. Clinical trials are slow (5-10 years to readout)
2. Interventions are priced for the wealthy (£2,000-5,000/year)
3. Low-income populations—who need them most—get excluded
4. Real-world data is scattered, unstructured, unusable

**Result:** Decades of delay before interventions reach people who could benefit. Meanwhile, healthspan inequality grows.

---

## Our Approach (200 words)

**Skip the bureaucracy. Deploy now, learn faster.**

We're building a decentralized network where:
- **People on low incomes** get subsidized access to evidence-based interventions
- **Real-world outcomes** are tracked systematically (not waiting for RCTs)
- **Community governance** ensures interventions serve actual needs, not commercial interests
- **Open data** accelerates research (no paywalls, no NDAs)

**This is "real-world RCTs" at scale:**
- 500 participants in Year 1, 5,000 by Year 3
- Standardized protocols (metformin, rapamycin, senolytics, NAD+ precursors)
- Biomarker tracking (quarterly bloodwork)
- Adverse event monitoring (48-hour reporting)
- Public data dashboard (anonymized)

**Why this works:**
- Faster than traditional trials (months, not years)
- More diverse population (not just healthy wealthy volunteers)
- Real-world effectiveness (not just efficacy in controlled settings)
- Direct benefit to participants (not just data extraction)
